Since October 2024, the City of Iqaluit began working on a series of impactful projects with University of 
Waterloo students to address critical municipal needs through innovative research and technology. These initial 
projects—focusing on software optimization for asset management, AI-driven predictive scheduling for water 
and sewer services, and AI-powered land use planning—are just the beginning of a long-term partnership aimed 
at tackling real-world infrastructure projects in Iqaluit utilizing large-language models. The City of Iqaluit aims to 
set a pioneering example for all of Nunavut by demonstrating how AI can be effectively leveraged to address 
municipal challenges, fostering innovation and resilience across the territory. Through these projects, Iqaluit 
strives to lead the way in AI-driven solutions for northern communities. 
 
Our goal is to continue this collaboration in the future, building on the momentum and insights gained in these 
early stages. By working closely with students and researchers, we envision expanding these efforts and 
identifying new project areas that align with Iqaluit's evolving priorities. This sustained partnership will not only 
benefit the City but will also offer students invaluable hands-on experience in developing AI/ML and LLM 
solutions for specifically for northern, remote, and uniquely challenging environments. Through continuous 
engagement, we hope to drive lasting change and further Iqaluit’s goals for sustainable growth and efficient city 
management. 
 
Outlined below are descriptions of the AI-focused projects currently underway with the University of Waterloo. 
To expand the impact of these initiatives and support future projects, we are seeking funding from CanNor. This 
support would enable us to drive these developments further, ensuring sustained progress and innovation in our 
work. 
 
Software Stack Analysis and Recommendation for Municipal Asset Management and Works Tasking 
This project aims to evaluate and improve Iqaluit's current software solutions for municipal asset management. 
Key objectives include analyzing existing tools, identifying system inefficiencies, and recommending an 
integrated software stack tailored to the city’s needs. The scope includes reviewing best practices, conducting 
an environmental scan, and developing a prototype solution. Expected deliverables are a comprehensive report 
on software gaps, a prototype stack or architecture plan, and a presentation summarizing findings and 
recommendations. 
 
AI/ML-Powered Trucked Water and Sewer Service Predictive Scheduling 
Focused on enhancing the efficiency of Iqaluit's trucked water and sewer services, this project seeks to 
minimize fuel usage and improve service reliability. Key objectives include developing predictive AI/ML models 
for household water consumption and optimizing delivery routes in real-time. The work involves data collection, 
algorithm development, and the creation of an app-based route guide. Deliverables include a predictive model, 
an optimized scheduling algorithm, a routing app, and a proof-of-concept demonstration with a technical report. 
 
AI/ML-Powered Land Use Planning 
This project addresses the need for optimized land use planning in Iqaluit by incorporating AI/ML to support 
sustainable urban growth. Objectives include creating AI-driven tools that analyze topography, geotechnical 
data, zoning laws, and community needs to develop optimized land use plans. The project entails data 
gathering, algorithm development, and generating actionable land use proposals. Deliverables include an 
AI/ML-powered land use planning tool, detailed land use plans with supporting analysis, and a presentation on 
methodology and results.

A. Need for the Project 
 
Description of Need:   
The City of Iqaluit is addressing critical gaps in municipal infrastructure management, essential service delivery, 
and urban planning to meet the growing Economic development demands of its residents while navigating the 
unique challenges of a northern, remote environment. Efficient municipal operations, particularly for essential 
services like water and sewer delivery, asset management, and sustainable land use planning, are v ital to 
fostering resilience and long term sustainable growth. Incorporating advanced AI/ML technologies into these areas 
presents a unique opportunity for Iqaluit to improve operational efficiency, reduce environmental impact, and 
optimize planning decisions tailored to the city's specific context. 
 
Supported Sector of the Economy:   
The project supports economic development and growth in the City of Iqaluit, particularly through enhancing 
municipal governance, public infrastructure, and urban planning. 
 
Why This Sector Needs Support:   
Economic growth in Iqaluit is hindered by unique logistical, environmental, and economic challenges associated 
with its northern and remote location. Limited resources, high transportation costs, harsh environmental 
conditions, and a growing population strain the city’s ability to deliver essential services and develop infrastructure 
effectively. Addressing these challenges is critical to creating a foundation for sustainable economic development. 
 
By integrating advanced AI/ML technologies, this initiative enables Iqaluit to optimize resource allocation, improve 
service efficiency, and make data-driven decisions. These advancements support the growth of local businesses, 
enhance workforce capacity, and attract investment in innovative solutions tailored to the city’s needs. Ultimately, 
these improvements strengthen Iqaluit's infrastructure and governance, providing a stable platform for long -term 
economic development and resilience

not constrained by the limited capacity of utilidor systems. By improving efficienc y and scalability, the project will 
empower Iqaluit to meet the demands of a growing population and facilitate sustainable urban development.

1. University of Waterloo Future Cities Institute (FCI):  The Future Cities Institute at UWaterloo, founded by

provide access to UWaterloo’s advanced computing resources with AI/ML capabilities, specialized software for AI

research university  (Research Information 2023), UWaterloo also brings expertise from Canada Excellence  
Research Chairs in Human -Centred Robotics, Machine Intelligence, Developmental Science, and Data 
Intelligence to advance project goals.

short courses (3 -4 days) led by top instructors in AI, allowing project team members to deepen their technical 
expertise. These courses are tailored to bridge academic AI knowledge with practical applications, pr oviding 
participants the skills to integrate new AI techniques directly into industry contexts. 
 
3. UWaterloo Faculty of Engineering:  As the largest engineering faculty in Canada, UWaterloo’s Faculty of 
Engineering supports the project by providing resources, expertise, and tools required for AI -driven urban 
solutions. The faculty fosters opportunities for students to engage with AI thr ough applied design projects, 
educational workshops, and hackathons, directly contributing to innovative project outcomes. 
 
4. UWaterloo Faculty of Mathematics:  The Faculty of Mathematics, home to the largest Computer Science 
department in Canada, offers additional resources and support for complex computational projects. With 
advanced capabilities in data science and AI, the faculty is integral in applying rigor ous data intelligence 
approaches to urban challenges.

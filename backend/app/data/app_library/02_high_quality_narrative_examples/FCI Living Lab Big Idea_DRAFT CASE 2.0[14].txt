Living Lab Initiative: A Transformative Approach to Sustainable Urban 
Innovation 
The Big Idea: 
The Future Cities Institute (FCI), founded by CAIVAN at the Faculty of Environment at the 
University of Waterloo, is launching a game-changing Living Lab Initiative that brings together 
academia, industry, government, and community to solve Canada’s most urgent urban challenges 
through sustainable, real-world innovation. 
This initiative directly addresses three national imperatives: 
1. Canada’s Global Competitiveness: Our economy depends on innovation, research 
commercialization, and talent development. The Living Lab will position Canada as a 
global leader in future-ready cities and sustainability solutions. 
2. The Role of Universities in Societal Progress: It is time for universities to be more 
visibly impactful. This initiative makes research transparent, actionable, and relevant—
directly benefitting our cities, industries, and governments. 
3. Training the Talent of Tomorrow: To lead in an increasingly complex world, 
tomorrow’s professionals must be able to connect ideas across disciplines and 
apply them in real-world contexts. The Future Cities Institute’s Living Lab places 
students at the heart of problem-solving—where research and teaching meets 
practice. By working alongside municipal, industry, and community partners on 
integrated urban challenges, students gain the critical thinking, adaptability, and 
practical insight needed to shape smarter, more sustainable cities. 
Vision and Objectives 
The Living Lab is an innovative platform for testing, implementing, and scaling long-term, 
transformational urban solutions. It will serve as a beacon for cities worldwide that seek health, 
prosperity and climate-resilient, and economically viable strategies for growth. 
By transforming our university into a living, breathing testbed, and by co-creating affordable 
housing solutions with Habitat for Humanity, we aim to: 
• Accelerate innovation in climate adaptation and urban sustainability 
• Train the next generation of interdisciplinary leaders 
• Showcase solutions that are scalable across municipalities  
• Foster strong, transparent partnerships between academia, industry, and community 
 
Two Core Components

1. University of Waterloo: Global Futures Village 
At the heart of the Future Cities Institute is a bold vision: to transform part of Waterloo’s north 
campus into a world-class Living Lab for urban innovation. The Global Futures Village, 
modeled on global exemplars like the Green Village at TU Delft, will serve as a dynamic hub 
where students, researchers, startups, municipalities, and industry leaders come together to test 
and refine urban solutions in a real-world environment—right here in Canada. 
Spanning 1,000 acres, the University of Waterloo already mirrors the scale and complexity of a 
mid-sized Canadian city. The Global Futures Village will formalize and expand this potential, 
positioning the campus as a national testbed for the sustainable, smart, and healthy cities of the 
future. 
People will live, work, learn and play in the Global Futures Village, which is focused on the 
implementation gap. New technology is not enough to transform cities. That’s why the Global 
Futures Village will also address the societal embrace, enabling legal and regulatory 
mechanisms, and business cases for urban innovations we develop and test.   
What Will the Living Lab Enable? 
• Urban Innovation Zones 
Test and refine next-generation systems in green infrastructure, energy, water, smart 
mobility, building science, digital planning, and affordable housing—including housing 
that doubles as live-in learning labs. 
• Climate Adaptation Pilot Projects 
Implement and monitor leading-edge solutions like bioswales, green roofs, permeable 
pavements, and tree canopy design, in collaboration with municipal and private sector 
partners. 
• Health and Urban Systems 
Leverage the future hospital on North Campus to explore the critical relationship between 
built environments, access to services, and population health. 
• Data-Driven Decision-Making 
Use Waterloo’s strengths in sensors, data science, and AI to evaluate interventions in real 
time, producing insights that can be applied in cities across Canada. 
• Social and Regulatory Testing Grounds 
The Living Lab will not only test technologies under real Canadian conditions (yes, 
including winter!)—it will also explore their social acceptability, economic feasibility, 
and the municipal and provincial policy frameworks needed to enable their success and 
scale. 
• Experiential Learning and Talent Development 
Students will be embedded in every facet of the lab—from co-op and Capstone to field 
schools and innovation challenges. They won’t just learn theory; they’ll help shape and 
test practical solutions with public and private partners, gaining the systems-thinking 
mindset and cross-sector fluency needed for leadership in a rapidly changing world. 
• A Resource for the Region 
The Living Lab will be a showcase and community asset. Local high school students will

participate in STEM-aligned field trips. Events and tours will welcome visitors and 
prospective students. And residents will participate in a unique 2-year residency—living 
in and contributing to the evolution of the lab itself. 
2. Habitat for Humanity Partnership: Building Affordable Futures Now 
In collaboration with Region of Waterloo municipalities and Habitat for Humanity, we are 
building research, training and replication roadmaps around a new model of affordable home 
ownership. Leveraging a $500 million investment in the City of Waterloo, and starting with a 25-
acre parcel of land in north Waterloo that will become an affordable community of over 1000 
households in the next five years, we will focus on:  
• Affordable, Sustainable Housing Builds: Model new techniques in energy-efficient and 
climate-resilient construction, on city-entrusted land. 
• Green Financing and Home Ownership Models: Explore new financial tools that 
empower individuals and communities. 
• Community-Engaged Research: Involve local residents, students, and faculty in 
designing inclusive, sustainable communities. 
• Data-Driven Decision-Making 
Use Waterloo’s strengths in sensors, data science, and AI to build scenarios and evaluate 
interventions in real time, producing insights that can be applied in cities across Canada. 
• Replication Roadmaps: All findings will be shared through toolkits, workshops, and 
digital platforms to support other cities in adopting these models. 
 
Four Key Research Priorities 
1. Climate Change Adaptation and Mitigation 
• Heat and flood risk mitigation – at the community and the household level 
• Climate-resilient design and natural infrastructure 
• Renewable energy integration 
2. Cities of the Future 
• Smart infrastructure and digital planning 
• Sustainable mobility and autonomous transportation 
• Livability and inclusive urban design 
3. Natural Resources and Environment 
• Circular economy and resource reuse 
• Urban biodiversity and water management 
• Land use for resilience and sustainability

4. Sustainable Transitions: Energy and Finance 
• Green infrastructure financing models 
• Campus-wide renewable energy transformation 
• Business models for municipal sustainability investments 
 
Why Invest in the Living Lab? 
A Platform for National and Global Impact 
• Innovation with Impact: Transformative ideas move from lab to real-world 
implementation. 
• Scalable and Replicable: Projects are designed for local application and national 
replication. 
• Community and Equity Focused: Tackles affordability, housing access, and inclusive 
design. 
• Talent Magnet: Prepares leaders who are fluent in tech, sustainability, policy, and

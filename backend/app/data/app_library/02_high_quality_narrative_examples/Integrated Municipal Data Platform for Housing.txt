Integrated Municipal Data Platform for Housing, Energy, and Climate Resilience 
A Proposal for the 2026 NFRF International Joint Initiative for Research Harnessing 
Disruptive Technologies 
The Challenge 
Municipalities across the Global North face an urgent coordination crisis. Departments 
operate in silos with separate data systems, planning processes, and implementation 
roadmaps—even when addressing deeply interconnected challenges like housing 
affordability, energy transition, and climate adaptation. This fragmentation creates 
systemic inefficiencies: duplicate data collection, conflicting priorities, missed 
opportunities for integrated solutions, costly misalignment between departments, and 
delayed decision-making, which are costly for all sectors of society. 
Recent policy pressures amplify this urgency. Local governments in Canada, the UK, and 
the Netherlands face budget constraints and mandates to demonstrate cost savings. At the 
same time, these municipalities grapple with housing crises requiring coordinated 
responses across planning, infrastructure, and social services, while also struggling to 
integrate energy, housing, and climate resilience planning at the operational level. Despite 
shared challenges, no adaptable framework exists to help municipalities of varying 
capacity and governance structures break down these silos. 
The cost of inaction is measurable: wasted resources, delayed climate action, housing 
shortages exacerbated by uncoordinated planning, and energy transitions that ignore 
housing stock realities. Municipalities need a revolutionary approach—not another rigid 
platform, but an adaptable toolkit that enables data integration and cross-departmental 
decision-making within diverse governance contexts. 
Our Approach: An Adaptable Municipal Integration Toolkit 
We propose to develop and validate an adaptable digital integration framework that 
enables municipalities to break down departmental silos and make coordinated decisions 
across housing, energy, and climate resilience. This is not a one-size-fits-all platform. 
Instead, we will create a modular toolkit with customizable components that 
municipalities can configure to their unique governance structures, data maturity levels 
and municipal capacity, and policy contexts. 
Three Priority Domains: 
1. Housing: Our municipal partners identify housing as the most urgent coordination 
challenge—requiring integration across planning, infrastructure, social services, 
and climate adaptation

2. Energy: Thermal systems, building performance, and grid planning require 
coordination between housing, transportation, and climate departments 
3. Climate Resilience: Adaptation planning demands integrated data on housing 
vulnerability, energy systems, and infrastructure capacity 
Technical Innovation: We will develop AI-powered data integration architecture and digital 
twin capabilities that can ingest heterogeneous municipal data, identify cross-
departmental dependencies, and surface coordination opportunities. The revolutionary 
element is not the technology alone—it's how the architecture enables new forms of 
municipal decision-making that were previously impossible due to siloed systems. 
Implementation Innovation: Recognizing that municipalities have vastly different 
capacities for digital transformation, we will develop an implementation framework with 
three tiers of adoption: lightweight integration for resource-constrained councils, moderate 
integration for mid-capacity municipalities, and advanced digital twin deployment for well-
resourced partners. This tiered approach ensures our research produces usable outcomes 
across diverse municipal contexts.

Pathway to Impact and SDG Alignment 
Direct SDG Contributions: 
• SDG 11 (Sustainable Cities and Communities): Primary focus—making 
municipalities more integrated, efficient, and sustainable 
• SDG 16 (Peace, Justice, and Strong Institutions): Increasing transparency and 
efficiency in local government decision making through integrated data and 
processes 
• Secondary Focus:  
o SDG 7 (Affordable and Clean Energy): Energy system coordination and 
building performance 
o SDG 13 (Climate Action): Integrated climate resilience planning 
 
Long-term Vision: Municipalities across OECD countries face similar silo challenges with 
housing, energy, and climate. By 2030, our research will produce the first adaptable 
framework proven across three governance contexts, positioning our partner cities as 
exemplars and creating a pathway for widespread adoption. We anticipate our toolkit will 
be particularly valuable for the majority of municipalities that lack resources for bespoke 
digital transformation but need practical solutions to coordination challenges. 
Stakeholder Engagement: Municipal partners will co-design the toolkit from inception, 
ensuring research priorities align with operational needs. We will establish steering 
committees in each partner city with representatives from housing, energy, climate, and

finance departments—embedding cross-departmental thinking into the research process 
itself. Non-academic partners will be full collaborators, not just test sites. 
This proposal directly responds to NFRF's call for disruptive technology addressing 
global challenges. Our innovation lies in creating adaptable infrastructure for 
municipal coordination—a capability that doesn't currently exist but which every city 
needs as they navigate interconnected housing, energy, and climate crises. With 
proven international expertise, genuine municipal partnerships, and a clear pathway 
from research to implementation, we are positioned to deliver transformative 
outcomes for sustainable cities worldwide.

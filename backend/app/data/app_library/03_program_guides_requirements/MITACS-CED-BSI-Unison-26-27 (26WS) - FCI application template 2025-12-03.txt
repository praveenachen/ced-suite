A-4.1a Background (What problem needs to be solved?) 
Provide an overview and describe the main activities of the partner organization. Explain the

Overview of partner organization  
The City of Edmonton’s Data Science and Research (DSR) team acts as a central problem-solving 
hub, collaborating with diverse internal municipal teams by accepting projects based on a 
structured evaluation process. It is dedicated to leveraging data and technology to enhance 
municipal services and improve quality of life for residents. This team leads initiatives in open data, 
artificial intelligence and predictive analytics, and collaborates with various stakeholders to drive 
innovation and transparency. Their work includes developing predictive models for emergency 
events, optimizing urban planning through data-driven insights, and fostering a culture of open 
government through accessible data portals.  
 
The team acts as a central problem-solving hub, collaborating with diverse internal municipal 
teams by accepting projects based on a structured evaluation process. This process scores 
requests against the City’s Corporate Business Plan and their potential to generate value 
(make/save money, reduce risk, save operationalizing the results, projects are prioritized and 
initiated. 
 
The DSR team emphasizes strong relationships, a well-defined problem, willing stakeholders, 
clarity on expected changes, and comfort with unpredictable results as critical for project success, 
all while maintaining a strong focus on data privacy and ethics. 
 
Description of current challenge  
The City of Edmonton faces the ongoing challenge of meeting its operational needs for public 
safety resource deployment and ultimately keeping the public safe. The City’s Data Science and 
Research (DSR) team has developed a public safety predictive analytics tool that leverages data to 
inform the proactive deployment of public safety resources.  
 
This project involves reviewing the current operational support structure to ensure optimal 
positioning for scaling and maintaining the application’s data science capabilities. Specifically, the 
DSR team needs to improve the accuracy and precision of existing predictive models that forecast 
calls for service, integrate diverse data sources to enrich these predictions and uncover deeper 
insights into public safety trends, and develop advanced features—particularly localized prediction 
capabilities—that provide more granular, actionable intelligence for resource deployment 
decisions.

The DSR team successfully developed and deployed the Unison Application, a sophisticated, 
unified platform designed to centralize and manage all critical City of Edmonton incidents as well 
as forecasting for the next day where the hotspot is. This powerful system achieves comprehensive 
situational awareness by integrating real-time data streams from eight diverse internal and external 
sources, providing a single source of truth for city operations and emergency management. 
  
  
The strategic roadmap for the ongoing development of the Unison Application focuses on three key 
priority areas to further enhance its capabilities and value: 
• Advanced Forecasting Capabilities: The DSR team is actively working on implementing 
next-generation forecasting models. These new models will move beyond simple historical 
trends by incorporating complex spatial and temporal relationships between various city 
grids—such as traffic flow, utility consumption, and environmental factors—to predict 
future incidents with greater accuracy and lead time. This capability will significantly 
improve proactive resource allocation and operational planning. 
• User Experience and Operational Enhancements: A core priority is continuous 
improvement based on actionable insights derived from direct user feedback. Planned 
enhancements include the addition of new, highly requested features focused on improving 
data accessibility, providing more intuitive and interactive data visualization tools, and 
streamlining workflows. These updates are designed to ensure the Unison platform remains 
an efficient and indispensable tool for all city personnel. 
• Incident Consolidation through Generative AI: The DSR team is pioneering the 
implementation of Generative Artificial Intelligence (Gen AI) to revolutionize the incident 
consolidation process. This new Gen AI approach will precisely consolidate high-volume, 
related incident calls at the granular call type level. This represents a major technological 
upgrade from the platform's current system, which relies on a heuristic AI approach that 
only matches and consolidates incidents at the less detailed higher occurrence type level. 
The move to Gen AI will dramatically increase the precision and fidelity of incident grouping, 
leading to faster, more accurate analysis and response. 
 
Impact of project 
 
The Unison project is a critical application for safety deployment, involving collaboration between 
internal and external teams. Enhancing its capabilities by providing more accurate forecasting—
through the consideration of location relationships and environmental factors—will not only 
improve immediate predictions but also allow for multi-day forecasting, facilitating better planning 
for upcoming weeks. Furthermore, improvements to the user interface will enable users to define 
their areas of interest, filtering the volume of information to present exactly what is relevant to their 
needs. Providing forecasts at a more granular, call-type level will also allow teams to focus on more 
specific and detailed information. In addition, by establishing clear metrics to measure UNISON's 
impact, the City will be able to quantify how the tool is contributing to making communities safer 
and continuously improving it based on real results. This goes beyond day-to-day operations by 
fundamentally strengthening the City's ability to proactively protect residents rather than 
responding to incidents after they occur.

The Unison project's core objectives are twofold: 
• To develop a robust forecasting model for the strategic deployment of safety resources. 
• To enhance system functionality to allow users to conduct customized data analysis. 
These objectives will be achieved through a data-driven, iterative methodology. The initial steps will

A-4.2  INTERNSHIP BENEFITS 
 
A-4.2a Benefits and Impacts to Canada 
If this project potentially impacts broader challenges that society or the industry faces, please 
describe how the project will help address these challenges. Why are these contributions important? 
 
The UNISON project addresses broader challenges that many cities and public safety organizations 
face in efficiently allocating limited resources to protect their communities. As municipalities 
across North America grapple with budget constraints, increasing service demands, and the need 
to demonstrate measurable outcomes, data-driven approaches to public safety resource 
deployment offer a path forward. By developing enhanced predictive models, integrating diverse 
data sources, and establishing robust performance metrics, this project contributes to the growing 
field of smart city analytics and evidence-based public safety practices. The focus on building a 
scalable data science framework and exploring advanced methodologies like MLOps and causal 
inference creates knowledge and approaches that can be adapted by other jurisdictions facing 
similar challenges.  
 
Ultimately, this work demonstrates how cities can leverage data science to move from reactive to 
proactive service delivery, improving both the efficiency of resource use and the safety outcomes 
for residents—challenges that are increasingly urgent for communities everywhere.

Mitacs will not approve a BSI application  if the intern listed on the proposal has a position of 
ownership, employment, or influence over the daily operations of the partner organization.

the partner org anization are also ineligible. Any other real or perceived conflict of interest (COI) - 
for example, past intern employment at the partner organization  - must be disclosed to 
Mitacs during the application process using  the Intern Eligibility and Conflict of Interest

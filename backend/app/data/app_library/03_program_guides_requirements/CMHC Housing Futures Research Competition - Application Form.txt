Section 6: Budget Justification
Attach a detailed budget with anticipated costs in relation to all project activities. As part of this funding competition, 
CMHC will support both direct and indirect costs of research. Indirect costs are defined as costs that are not directly 
attributable to the project but necessary for its support. These may include, for example, facility use, administrative 
services, library and IT support. Acceptable expense categories for “direct costs” include:  
• Personnel costs (such as salaries, stipend, wages of students/trainees)
• Travel for field work and stakeholder engagement or collaboration
• Equipment and supplies (including specialized tools, software, lab materials, consumables)
• Consultants/subcontracts (external expertise or services contributing to the project activities)
– No more than 30% of the total project cost may be spent on consultants/subcontracts
• Participant costs (honoraria, incentives, or reimbursements for research participants)
• Dissemination (costs for translation, publishing, meetings and KM activities)
• Budget justification should clearly link costs to project objectives, ensure accurate calculations, and reference
institutions policies, as and where applicable.
Please carefully review Section 6: Budget Justification under Section X: Format and requirements 
for Proposals of the Call for Proposal Guide to submit budget and corresponding justification.
